# Proof of Origin

AI-generated images are everywhere and indistinguishable from real ones — metadata gets stripped, screenshots erase history, and a single re-save cuts any connection to the source. Proof of Origin fixes this: every image generated through the app gets three independent invisible watermarks (a frequency-domain fingerprint that survives AI regeneration, a neural steganographic layer that survives JPEG and resizing, and a metadata signature), a zero-knowledge proof that binds the watermark ID, image hash, and DINOv2 perceptual hash together and is verified by a smart contract on Base Sepolia at registration time — so the blockchain itself vouches for the honest watermarking, not just stores a hash — and the full provenance record anchored permanently to Arweave. To verify, you upload any version of the image — original, screenshot, crop, re-encoded JPEG — and the app reads the watermarks, queries the chain, and tells you whether it is the exact original or a transformed derivative; a trustless verify button queries the contract directly from the browser with no backend involved. This makes the system the first compliance-ready implementation of the EU AI Act's requirement that AI-generated content be machine-detectable as synthetic — built on a secure, defence-in-depth architecture where an attacker must defeat three independent watermark layers simultaneously, and where a compromised backend cannot fabricate an on-chain ZK proof retroactively.
